Local Date Ideas

= Utah State University Activity

  • Play magic, dnd, and other card / board games at Game Grid
  • Make charcuterie board and watch sunrise/sunset at Hyrum Gibbon Mt. Logan Park
  • Cooking class at Love To Cook
  • Go to the D.I. and find each other an outfit for under $15 dollars
  • Local Music at The Cache
  • Jazz night (live music, food, dancing) at Elite Hall in Hyrum
  • Learn to Ski / Snowboard at Beaver Mountain - Learn-To-Turn
  • Volunteer to walk dogs / play with cats at Cache Humane Society
  • Weekend brunch at Herm's Inn if there is a wait, take a walk to first dam
  • Get a drink from local coffee shops
    - Westside
    - Cafe Ibis
  • Walk the Logan River Trail. You can make a loop with the Highland Trail. (~3.5 mi) Climb at Elevation
  • Try something new and rent something from campus recreation. (ie. paddle boards, skis/snowboards, mountain bikes, camping gear, etc)
  • Around holidays, take a picture with Santa at the mall
  • Try great vegan food at Bloom
  • Visit Tony Grove Lake. There is a small trial around the lake, paddle boarding, kayacking, or trail heads to Naomi Peak or White Pine (White pine is also a great beginner backpacking trail)
  • Create a playlist together and take a scenic drive to Bear Lake. Enjoy local raspberry shakes. Raspberry Days (August 3rd-5th 2023)
  • Ice Skating at George S. Eccles Ice Center
  • Explore Logan's Art Scene. Visit the Nora Eccles Harrison Museum of Art at Utah State University or stroll through the Cache Valley Center for the Arts in downtown Logan to enjoy local art and performances
  • Enjoy a night at the Utah Theatre. Catch a movie or live performance at the historic Utah Theatre in downtown Logan. The theatre has been beautifully restored and offers a unique atmosphere for a night out.
  • Visit the American West Heritage Center. This living history museum showcases the history of the American West. You and your date can take a tour, watch a live demonstration, and learn about the pioneers who settled in the area.
  • Hike to Wind Caves. Take a hike up to the Wind Caves in Logan Canyon. The trail is moderate in difficulty and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains. Or take a less traveled hike across the canyon, Crimson Trail. Crimson trail lines the top of spectacular cliffs and offers a different view of the Wind Caves.
  • Visit Zootah and have a picnic at Willow Park after
  • Play Horseshoe at Willow Horseshoe Park
  • Visit Red Balloon Toys and find a project that you can do together
  • Sled down the hill at Old Main on campus
  • Get in shape together. Run the stairs on Old Main followed by a jog on the canal trail to First Dam
  • Get a paint set and paint some of Logan’s beautiful scenery. Dry canyon, or first or second dam would be great options
  • Participate in a scavenger hunt
  • Go fishing. Logan River is one of the best places to go fishing in the state, and you can find a variety of fish,including brown trout, rainbow trout, and cutthroat trout
  • Go swimming at Porcupine Reservoir
  • Take a polar bear plunge at second dam
  • Go to the Logan LDS Temple. If you are a member of the LDS church, you can visit the Logan Temple and take a guided tour. The temple is a beautiful and peaceful place to spend some time.
  • Stargaze up Logan canyon
  • Go to Color Me Mine and paint a pot
  • Take a paint class at Image This
  • Go to the Farmer’s market and buy fresh ingredients to cook a meal together
  • Take a pottery class at Logan Fine Art Gallery
  • Attend a sporting event at Utah State University
  • Go on a geocaching adventure
  • Attend a rodeo at the fairgrounds
  • Golfing at Logan Golf and Country Club
  • Snowshoe Green Canyon
  • Axe throwing, bowling, arcade, rollarblading at Fun Park
  • sk80’s at Fun Park
  • Try snowmobiling at Beaver Creek Lodge
  • Take highway 30 to Honeyville and enjoy Crystal Hot Springs
  • Go on a drive to Preson, ID and visit where Napoleon Dynamite was filmed
  • Go night skiing for 25$ at Cherry Peak Resort
  • Visit the Bird Refuge
  • Paddle board in Mantua
  • Visit The Book Table - a bookstore that offers a wide variety of books, toys, and gifts.
  • Attend Crumb Brother’s Sunday Brunch
  • Rock Climb at the Arc's Climing Wall
  • Try paddle board yoga
  • Explore the other side of the valley and hike the Ridgeline of the Wellsville Mountains
  • Volunteer at a soup kitchen
